Interdisciplinary Projects & Research
Projects

Projects that connect computer engineering with other domains and real-world problems.

Smart Campus Energy Dashboard

A conceptual project where I explored how IoT data and web dashboards can help a campus reduce electricity usage.

  • Objectives: Visualize energy consumption across buildings and highlight peak usage hours.
  • Methodology: Used dummy sensor readings stored in a database and displayed them using charts on a web page.
  • Outcomes: Identified patterns like heavy usage during specific time slots and proposed schedule adjustments.
  • Challenges: Designing charts that would be understandable for non-technical users.
Mental Well-Being Check-In App

A simple app idea to allow students to log their mood and track stress patterns over time.

  • Objectives: Provide a private and low-friction way for students to reflect on their mood daily.
  • Methodology: Built screens for mood logging and used basic charts to show weekly trends.
  • Outcomes: Helped me understand the importance of data privacy and responsible handling of personal information.
  • Challenges: Choosing the right questions so that the app felt helpful rather than intrusive.
Traffic-Aware Bus Scheduling Prototype

A Python-based prototype focusing on improving bus arrival timings for students using simple traffic assumptions.

  • Objectives: Reduce waiting time at bus stops during peak college hours.
  • Methodology: Wrote scripts to calculate travel times between stops and simulated different schedules.
  • Outcomes: Proposed a revised time table which, in simulation, reduced average waiting time.
  • Challenges: Dealing with real-life constraints like fixed bus routes and limited vehicles.

Global Awareness & Ethical Considerations
Ethics

How global issues and ethics shape the way I think about technology.

Data Privacy in Student Applications

While working on ideas like the mental well-being app and finance tools, I realised that even ‚Äúsimple‚Äù data such as mood logs or spending patterns can reveal a lot about a person.

  • Issue: Storing personal and behavioural data that could be misused if not handled carefully.
  • Ethical Concern: Risk of exposing sensitive information or using it without clear consent.
  • Response: Minimising collected data, avoiding unnecessary personal identifiers, and keeping the user informed about how data is used.

Global Perspective:

Technology today reaches users across different countries and laws. This makes me think about:

  • Being transparent about what data is stored and why.
  • Designing settings that default to safer and more private choices.
  • Considering performance and accessibility for users with limited devices or connectivity.

I want my future projects to balance innovation with responsibility, instead of only focusing on features.
